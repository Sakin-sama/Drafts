I receive this glimpse through you,
This glimpse into what you have,
That which I never will,
Or at least never did;
This glimpse to you, from you, of you.

A love unlike any other,
So bright that looking directly will burn thine eyes,
Like the glorious Sun at midday rise.
Through the gap in curtains I peek,
At the love you have, that which all seek,
Somedays near, some far,
Far, far away, high up
On the pedestal I placed.

On those days, I realize it for what it is,
Worship isn't love, it's
Distance.
Dressed as devotion.

Society calls them lovers:
A pair of individuals who love each other.
But they forget about lovers:
Those who love love,
Even if they don't feel it themselves.
Lovers? No. Lover's.

They say love is for he and she, but
What about all who lie in-between.
What about them?
What about you and you whom love so desperately?
Away,
From prying eyes,
From public opinion,
From religous doctrine,
From political law.

Your gift to me,
From lovers to lover's,
A gift not given but well received,
Like lenses that make the blurry clear,
To see the world through a different lens,
The world-lens not of one half of a pair of lovers,
But of one piece of many lover's.

That in which I may not partake but only view,
May only see, never
Touch, never
Experience.
You, you two, are
Michelangelo's David; behind banister and line,
And I, the child reaching forwards, afar,
With a twinkle in the eye,
Awoken as if he'd eaten
The very Fruit of Eden.

You are every painting, every sculpture, every bust
In the Louvre, in the Met,
But you are so, so much more,
For an art gallery could never be as unique as you.
